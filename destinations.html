Ontario, Canada


 About                                                                               Interesting Facts

Canada is an amazing destination with some of the most friendly people I have met along my travels. The trip we took started in lewiston, NY with a wonderful park overlooking the Canadian border. There is also a wonderful concert venue here. After a quick picnic, we then traveled across the border to Niagara falls. The downtown city comes across a bit cheesy, but that is made up for by the beautiful views and the excellent food.
Niagara falls is one of the most stunning things you can ever see and hopefully you arrive on a beautiful day. One suggestions is to 100% take the boat ride that brings you out into the falls. the view is simply amazing, but be prepared to get drenched! The rumbling of the falls can be heard from all over and we found ourselves there just taking them in for hours! At night the falls night up so its great to comeback after the sun goes down. Continuing our viewing of the falls, we then ate at The Keg Steakhouse, which provided both amazing food and a wonderful dining view of the falls from above. Make sure you visit during the summer, as winters are simply miserable here!

  • World's Longest Street
    Yonge Strees in Ontario has a distance of over 1178 miles. It was declared by the Guinness Book of World Records in 1998.

  • World's Longest Freshwater Beach
    Located northwest of Toronto, Wasaga Beach stretches 8.6 miles long.

  • Largest Sony Big Screen
    Placed inside of the Rogers Centre in Toronto, the screen measures 33 feet by 110 feet.

Split, Croatia


 About                                                                               Interesting Facts

Believe it or not, Croatia's food was simply better then Italy here. The water was special and the breads showed it in their unique flavor. The city is water front and is simply beautiful. Some tips for this city are to dedicate enough time to walk around the historic area, and take a boat ride off the coast out into the blue water and see the city from at sea. Paris, France


 About                                                                               Interesting Facts

My time in Paris began and ended with the Eiffel Tower. During the day, the tower is magnifcant and you NEED to go all that way up to the top! I mean, why stop at the lower level when there’s sky high champagne at the top! Yet, as beautiful as it is during the day, the nighttime offers a spectacular light show to be enjoyed by all! My travel buddies and I picked up a bottle of wine and relaxed enjoying the show from the grassy field below the tower with thousands of other people.
While in Paris, I made sure to check out the Musee du Louvre. You could spend your entire trip exploring the Louvre. Since we were trying to hit as much of Paris as possible, we made a B line for the Mona Lisa to see one of the most famous works of all time. YES SHE DOES FOLLOW YOU! Next time we hope to explore more! Fun Fact: FREE ENTRY ON FRIDAYS!
Another historical place on my list to see was the Notre Dame Cathedral. This is located right in the center of the city and is simply magnificent. The stained glass is some of the most beautiful in all of Europe! Located nearby is the less famous, but equally beautiful Sainte Chapelle. It is a must stop for any traveler trying to see something a bit off the mainstream trail.

  • The Eiffel Tower
    The tower was built in 1889 for the World Fair and only expected to be standing for 20 years.

  • Army
    It was the French army who came up with camouflage. The French began to wear camouflage in 1915 during WWI.

  • Public Transportation
    Paris has the fourth largest public transportation system in the world. The Paris Metro consists of 302 stations.

Israel


 About                                                                               Interesting Facts

I went to Israel on a birthright trip in June of 2014. It was one of the best experiences of my life. Birthright is a free cultural educational trip to Israel for young Jewish individuals.
With the trip lasting a short ten days, the staff really makes sure to pack in as much as possible. Every place we went was always exciting teaching us something new. I got to experience things that I would never have expected for myself. Experiences like floating in the Dead Sea, hiking up the very tall Mount Masada at 3 am to catch the sunrise, and even sleeping out in the desert in the bedouin tents next to camels made me realize how truly beautiful the world is. This trip is something that I can never forget. With a giant group of 40 strangers from all over the country on a trip for 10 days, you wouldn't think that people still keep in touch, but we do. This trip gave us more than sight seeing and education on the Jewish culture, it gave us a family too.

  • The Dead Sea
    The Dead Sea is the lowest place on earth with an extremely high salt concentration forcing everything to float.

  • Development
    The world's first jellyfish repellent was developed by an Israeli company.

  • Tel Aviv
    Every Tuesday night people gather for a giant rollerblading party in Tel Aviv.

Italy:

Florence, Tuscany, Rome, The Vatican, Venice, Amalfi


                               About                                                 Interesting Facts

2014 was my biggest trevel year. I studied abroad over the summer in the city of Florence. Every weekend myself and well as other students would travel Europe hopping country to country. Travel in Europe is extremely easy and cheap. I flew a total of 11 planes that summer and will never forget this once in a lifetime experience to be able to go to school in another country.
Let’s start with the city of Florence itself. The city boasts beautiful architecture, famous paintings and sculptures (such as Michelangelo’s David), the renowned Duomo, cobblestone roads, the Italian leather markets- all of which is all literally at your fingertips. Walking across the Ponte Vecchio, rubbing the Florentine Boar for good luck, meeting in front of the beautiful Duomo, looking at all the real Italian leather all just becomes daily routine. It’s surreal. You’re surrounded by all this beauty that America just doesn’t have. By week three you almost have to remind yourself to soak it all in because seeing all these beautiful sites almost becomes too normal.
Thinking about the food now is literally making my mouth water.  Whether you indulge in a traditional Florentine steak or just enjoy an authentic Italian style pizza, you really can’t go wrong with anything you choose. Then follow that meal with a gelato from you’re favorite gelateria where your mouth will think it is in heaven.
One great experience to consider when traveling to Italy is a cooking class! I literally got learn how to cook real Italian meals in Italy and how to pair them with wine. I would have never thought that I could earn actual college credits towards my bachelor's degree eating the most amazing foods in the world and having three glasses of wine to go along with the food.
If I could go back and change one thing about studyng abroad, I would choose the long semester over the short summer. I expected living in another country, for what seemes to me like a long while, would be extremely difficult. I did not think that I could be away from family that long. The time flew by and I was so sad to leave. If I could do it all over again I would choose to do so in the blink of an eye.
One thing you definitely do a lot of in Europe is walking. Between exploring the city and going to classes everyday my friends and I averaged walking about 10 miles daily without realizing it.
There is a group in Europe called Bus2Alps. They organized the most amazing and fun filled trips around Europe with great deals specially made for students. Through Bus2Alps I was able to see the spectacular cities of Venice, Rome, Vatican City, the vineyards of Tuscany, the island of Capri, Positano, Pompeii, Assisi, Cinque Terre, and Sienna.
I was fortunate enough to be able to explore the Blue Grotto, cliff jumping near and relaxing on the Amalfi beaches, walking through the ruins of Pompeii, the art in the Sistine Chapel and the Vatican, the Collosseum, St. Peter’s Basilica, the Trevi Fountain, the Pantheon, the Catacombs, and so much much more. Definitely trips that I would do a million times again. Italy is full of so much history and its all there for you to see.

  • The Name
    Italy comes from the word Italia, which means "calf land". This is thought to be because the bull was symbol for Southern Italian tribes.

  • Historic
    Italy is known to own the most historic masterpieces in the world.

  • Proud Chefs
    As soon as Mcdonalds landed on the Italian terf in Rome, people would hover outside of the restaurant to hand out free spaghetti to remind people of their cullinary heritage.

  • Cheese
    Italy originated Parmesean, gorgonzola, mozzarella, provolone, ricotta and many other cheeses.

  • Vatican City
    The Vatican is an independent state within Italy. It is the only nation in the World to lock gates to the city at night. They have their own money, an army of historic Swiss Guards, radio and TV stations, and more.

  • Earthquakes
    Italy suffers from the most earthquakes out of all of the regions in Europe.

  • Volcanos
    Italy's peninsula is on top of the fault line, thus creating the most volcanos in Europe.

  • Popular
    Over 50 million people visit Italy every year. Tourism contributes to 63% of Italy's national income.

  • Hideout
    During WWII, many Jews fled to Italy to hide in the ancient catacombs

Barcelona, Spain


                               About                                                 Interesting Facts

This iconic party city did not let down with the night life scene! Beach front clubs open their doors around midnight and the fun goes until very early in the morning hours. It makes me think; how do people around here go to work the next day?? Maybe it’s the mid-day break that helps them get through the day! One tip is to do one of the bar/club crawls. This helps avoid the long lines and cover charges. As well, the one we were one bussed us to a bar on the outskirts of the city that gave us a wonderful view overlooking the city lights from above.
One thing people may fail to notice when getting caught up in the Barcelona night life is the beauty and history of the city. The number one stop is for sure La Sagrada Familia. This old charge has been built over the past 100 years and is still not finished! The amazing thing about this is how the churches architecture and design are a real life freestanding time line of history! The Magic Fountain show was up next and is a must see for any tourist. The show going from 7-9 pm brings together water, lights and music is an epic display. Lastly, you need to take a walk down La Rambla, the famous shopping street of Barcelona. This is a place to wander, window shop and simply get lost in the magic

  • Kingdom
    Spain has been officially declared as "The Kingdom of Spain". It is the second largest country in Europe.

  • Public Nudity
    Public nudity is legal in the country of Spain.

  • Eiffel Tower
    The Eiffel Tower was originally planned to be built in barcelona.
